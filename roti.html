RECIPE

Fatima Sydow's Flaky Roti

INGREDIENTS

  • 1kg Cake Flour
  • 2 tsp of salt
  • 60ml sunflower oil
  • 650-700ml cold water
  • 200g soft butter or margarine
  • Extra flour for dishing

METHOD

Combine flour and salt in a bowl. Pour in some of the cold water and mix using your hands. Add the rest of the water, little by little. You may need between 650-700ml water. Mix into a soft dough.You can sprinkle flour and rub your hands together to remove sticky dough. Continue to knead...Add more flour if necessary. When finished kneading, let the dough rest for 30minutes.

Roll out the rested dough on a very lightly floured surface. Smear butter all over the rolled out dough and sprinkle with flour.Roll the dough into a thick rope. Dab the rope of dough with oil to prevent the dough drying out. Stretch the dough into a even longer rope. If you leave the dough to rest for 5-10 minutes it will be easier to stretch the dough.

Pull off pieces of the dough and gently stretch to mak individual rope, one side clockwise and the other anti-clockwise. Fold one half on top of the other. Continue to do this until all the dough is finished. Top each ball of dough with more oil. Place each ball in a plate to rest for 20 minutes, making sure they are covered with plastic wrap.

Roll out the balls to make circles(20cm in diameter). Place into a heated, heavy base pan. Fry both sides, flipping them over occasionally. You will notice the roti is puffing. Remove from pan and pat the roti between your palms to fluft

ENJOY!



Source: Salwaa's Cape Malay Cooking
